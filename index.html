Betty White

American actress and comedian

Here's a time line of Betty White's life:

  • 1922 - Born in Oak Park, Illinois
  • 1949 - She began appearing as a co-host on the show called Hollywood on Television
  • 1951 - She was nominated for her first Emmy for 'best actress' though she did not win
  • 1952 - She took over hosting Hollywood on Television
  • 1953 - White along with George Tibbles and Don Fedderson, created a new show called Life with Elizabeth.
  • 1959 - White made her professional stage debut in a week-long production of the play, Third Best Sport, at the Ephrata Legion Star Playhouse in Pennsylvania
  • 1962 - She made her feature film debut as fictional Kansas Senator Elizabeth Ames Adams in Advise and Concent
  • 1963 - She married Allen Ludden who was the host of the tv game show Password
  • 1973 - White began making several appearances in the The Mary Tyler Moore Show
  • 1975 - White won her first emmy in her role on Mary Tylor More
  • 1976 - White won her second emmy in her role on Mary Tylor More
  • 1983 - White became the first woman to win a Daytime Emmy Award in the category of Outstanding Game Show Host, for the NBC entry Just Men!
  • 1985 - White scored her second signature role and the biggest hit of her career as Rose Nylund on The Golden Girls
  • 2006 - White joined the soap opera The Bold and the Beautiful in the role of Ann Douglas
  • 2008 - She appeared on The Oprah Winfrey Show, taking part in the host's Mary Tyler Moore Show reunion special alongside every surviving cast member of the series
  • 2010 - A grassroots campaign on Facebook called 'Betty White to Host SNL (Please)' was approaching 500,000 members when NBC confirmed she would
  • 2011 - The Betty White Calendar was published featuring photos from White's career and with various animals
  • 2012 - Betty White's 90th Birthday Party, aired on NBC and featured appearances of many stars whom White worked with over the years. It also had a message from then sitting president Barack Obama.
  • 2018 - White's career was celebrated in a PBS documentary called Betty White: First Lady of Television.
  • 2021 - Just before White's death, it was announced that a new documentary-style film about her, Betty White: A Celebration would be released in U.S. theatres on her 100th birthday

The outpouring of love, the amazing amount of donations being given to all types of animal organizations is such a tribute to how Betty lived her life. As Betty would say, 'Thank you from the bottom of my heart.'

-- Her friend and longtime agent Jeff Witjas tells PEOPLE magazine about the donations in honor of her 100th Birthday
